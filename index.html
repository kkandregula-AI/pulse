How it actually works

With the flash on, your fingertip glows red. Each heartbeat pushes a little more blood through your finger, which subtly changes how much light gets back to the camera. Spandana measures that flicker, finds each beat, and turns the spacing between beats into your heart rate.

PPG is not ECG

PPG · what this does

  • Optical — camera + flash
  • Gives heart rate (bpm)
  • Rough HRV on a clean signal
  • Wellness awareness

ECG · what this can't do

  • Electrical — needs electrodes
  • Shows the heart's waveform
  • Can flag rhythm disorders
  • A camera can't sense this

The iPhone catch

Steady readings need the flash on. Android Chrome lets a web app control the torch, so Spandana turns it on for you. iPhone Safari does not give web apps torch control — so on iPhone you'll get a warning, and you'll need bright surroundings and a firm, still fingertip, or the reading won't settle.

Getting a good reading

Cover the whole lens, press gently but firmly, stay still, and breathe normally. Watch the signal-quality bar — save only when it's green. Cold hands and movement are the usual reasons a reading won't lock.

Privacy

The camera feed never leaves your phone — frames are analysed on-device and discarded. Readings are stored only in this browser. The optional AI insight sends just your numbers (not video) to Anthropic using your own key.
